  Key Innovations Section: This is a major section. Use a two-column layout with expandable cards or tall cards with room for detail. 8 cards:

  1. Robust 3-Stage Preprocessing vs Standard Scaling
    - Problem: Financial data has fat tails — a single extreme day can distort mean/std and corrupt StandardScaler.
    - Our approach: Winsorize (clip to 1st/99th percentile) → RobustScaler (uses median and IQR instead of mean/std) → MinMax to [0,1].
    - Effect: Outlier-resistant normalization that's perfectly invertible. Training bounds saved and applied identically at test time — zero data leakage.
  2. Sparse Denoising Autoencoder vs PCA
    - Problem: Most teams use PCA for dimensionality reduction. PCA is strictly linear — it cannot capture smile dynamics, skew, or term structure curvature in swaption
  surfaces.
    - Our approach: Nonlinear autoencoder with denoising (random 15% masking) and L1 sparsity.
    - Effect: Learns a nonlinear manifold that captures complex surface structure PCA misses. Denoising training directly enables Task B (missing data imputation) — the AE
  naturally fills gaps because it was trained to reconstruct from masked inputs. L1 sparsity yields interpretable latent factors — each dimension corresponds to a distinct
  market factor.
  3. ELU Bottleneck vs ReLU
    - Problem: ReLU activations at the bottleneck layer cause "dying neurons" — latent dimensions that collapse to zero and never recover, wasting capacity.
    - Our approach: ELU activation at the bottleneck (allows small negative values).
    - Effect: All 20 latent dimensions remain active throughout training. Combined with L1 sparsity, we get selective sparsity (some dims near-zero for specific inputs)
  without permanent death.
  4. Fixed QORC Ensemble vs Trained Quantum Circuits
    - Problem: Training quantum circuit parameters with only 494 samples leads to overfitting and barren plateaus.
    - Our approach: Reservoir computing paradigm — fixed random photonic circuits + trained classical readout.
    - Effect: Avoids barren plateaus entirely. Quantum features computed once and cached — no re-simulation every epoch. Training is fast (~20 min total).
  5. Ensemble Diversity via Physics, Not Random Seeds
    - Problem: Classical ensembles use different random seeds of the same model — limited diversity.
    - Our approach: Three reservoirs with different photon numbers (2, 3, 4) creating qualitatively different Fock spaces.
    - Effect: Pairwise correlations (2 photons), cubic interactions (3 photons), and quartic interactions (4 photons) — fundamentally different nonlinear feature families that
   cannot be replicated by different seeds of the same function class.
  6. Phase Encoding via Sigmoid → [0, 2π]
    - Problem: Challenge prohibits amplitude encoding. Need a mapping from real-valued latent features to phase parameters.
    - Our approach: Sigmoid maps to [0, 1], scaled to [0, 2π].
    - Effect: Smooth, differentiable, exploits full optical periodicity. Gradients flow through during head training.
  7. Predict in Latent Space, Not Price Space
    - Problem: Directly predicting 224 prices is high-dimensional and noisy.
    - Our approach: Predict the 20-dim latent code, then decode to prices via the frozen AE decoder.
    - Effect: 20-dim prediction is dramatically easier. The decoder guarantees all outputs respect the learned surface manifold — no arbitrage-violating predictions possible.
  8. Combined Latent + Surface Loss
    - Formula: L = MSE(z_pred, z_true) + 0.1 × MSE(surface_pred, surface_true)
    - Why both: Latent loss gives smooth, direct gradients early in training. Surface loss provides end-to-end accuracy signal in price space. The 0.1 weight prevents the
  noisier surface loss from dominating.
    - Effect: Gradients flow through the frozen AE decoder back to the head — end-to-end training signal without requiring decoder parameter updates.
