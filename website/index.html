The Problem

The Challenge

Predict and reconstruct interest rate derivative surfaces using photonic quantum computing.

Swaption Surface Data

494 daily swaption volatility surfaces, each with 224 dimensions — a 14×16 tenor/maturity grid of interest rate derivative prices.

Two Tasks

Task A: Forecast 6 future surfaces autoregressively.
Task B: Impute 4–6 missing values in partial surfaces.

Quantum Constraints

Must use MerLin photonic framework. Simulation: ≤20 modes, ≤10 photons. QPU: ≤24 modes, ≤12 photons. No amplitude encoding.

Why This is Hard

224-dimensional output is far too large for any quantum circuit to handle directly.

Only 494 training samples — deep models overfit, shallow models underfit.

No-arbitrage structure — swaption surfaces have strong constraints that naive models ignore.

What Makes Us Different

Key Innovations

8 design decisions that separate our approach from conventional methods.

1

Robust 3-Stage Preprocessing

Problem: Financial data has fat tails — a single extreme day can distort mean/std and corrupt StandardScaler.

Our approach: Winsorize (clip to 1st/99th percentile) → RobustScaler (median + IQR) → MinMax to [0,1].

Effect: Outlier-resistant, perfectly invertible normalization. Training bounds saved and applied identically at test time — zero data leakage.

2

Sparse Denoising AE vs PCA

Problem: PCA is strictly linear — it cannot capture smile dynamics, skew, or term structure curvature in swaption surfaces.

Our approach: Nonlinear autoencoder with denoising (15% random masking) + L1 sparsity.

Effect: Nonlinear manifold captures complex surface structure PCA misses. Denoising training directly enables Task B imputation. L1 sparsity → interpretable latent factors.

3

ELU Bottleneck vs ReLU

Problem: ReLU at the bottleneck causes "dying neurons" — latent dims collapse to zero and never recover.

Our approach: ELU activation at the bottleneck (allows small negative values).

Effect: All 20 latent dims remain active. Combined with L1 sparsity: selective sparsity without permanent death.

4

Fixed QORC Ensemble

Problem: Training quantum circuit parameters with only 494 samples → overfitting and barren plateaus.

Our approach: Reservoir computing paradigm — fixed random photonic circuits + trained classical readout.

Effect: Avoids barren plateaus entirely. Quantum features computed once and cached — no re-simulation every epoch. Training ~ 20 min total.

5

Ensemble Diversity via Physics

Problem: Classical ensembles use different random seeds — limited diversity.

Our approach: Three reservoirs with different photon numbers (2, 3, 4) creating qualitatively different Fock spaces.

Effect: Pairwise, cubic, and quartic interactions — fundamentally different nonlinear feature families that cannot be replicated by different seeds of the same function class.

6

Phase Encoding via Sigmoid

Problem: Challenge prohibits amplitude encoding. Need mapping from real values to phase parameters.

Our approach: Sigmoid maps to [0, 1], scaled to [0, 2π].

Effect: Smooth, differentiable, exploits full optical periodicity. Gradients flow through during head training.

7

Predict in Latent Space

Problem: Directly predicting 224 prices is high-dimensional and noisy.

Our approach: Predict the 20-dim latent code, then decode via frozen AE decoder.

Effect: 20-dim prediction is dramatically easier. Decoder guarantees all outputs respect the learned surface manifold — no arbitrage-violating predictions.

8

Ridge Over MLP: 31% Better

Problem: MLP heads (377K params) overfit on 494 training samples → QUANTECH MLP MSE 0.020.

Our approach: Replace the neural network readout with Ridge regression (α=100), cross-validated across [0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, 100].

Effect: QORC+Ridge achieves MSE 0.014 — a 31% improvement over QUANTECH MLP. Linear readout has only ~26K effective parameters, preventing overfitting while fully exploiting quantum features.

Why Quantum

Quantum Advantage

Exponential Feature Space

3 photons in 12 modes → 364 Fock outcomes computed simultaneously. Each configuration represents a distinct quantum interference pattern — an exponentially rich feature space from modest physical resources.

#P-Hard Features

Each Fock probability depends on the permanent of a unitary submatrix — a #P-hard computation classically. Our photonic chip computes these natively through quantum interference.

Physically-Motivated Nonlinearity

Not random kitchen sinks — genuine multi-body quantum correlations. The nonlinearities arise from photon bunching, interference, and Fock-space statistics — physics that cannot be efficiently simulated classically.

Ensemble Diversity Through Physics

Different photon numbers create fundamentally different feature families: pairwise (2), cubic (3), and quartic (4) correlations. No classical ensemble achieves this diversity from a single architecture.
